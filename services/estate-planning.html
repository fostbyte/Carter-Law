Why Estate Planning Matters

Without proper estate planning, your assets may not be distributed according to your wishes, your family may face unnecessary taxes and legal complications, and important decisions about your healthcare may be left to others. Estate planning gives you control over these important matters and provides security for your loved ones.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a will if I have a trust?

Yes, you typically need a "pour-over" will even with a trust to handle any assets not transferred to the trust and to name guardians for minor children.

What's the difference between a will and a trust?

A will takes effect after death and goes through probate, while a trust can take effect immediately and typically avoids probate, providing more privacy and efficiency.

When should I update my estate plan?

Review your estate plan every 3-5 years or after major life events like marriage, divorce, births, deaths, or significant changes in assets.

How much does estate planning cost?

Costs vary based on complexity, but it's much less expensive than the potential costs of not having a proper estate plan in place.
